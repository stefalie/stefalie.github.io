Vulkan SPH Particle Fluid

2021–2023

For my work at VirtaMed, I Independently developed position-based SPH (smoothed-particle hydrodynamics) particle fluid for surgical simulation. It features such particle properties as viscosity, surface tension, and adhesion. The GPU implementation animates and mixes several 100k blood, bile fluid, and water particles in real-time. To robustly collide the fluid against constantly deforming organs, a signed distance field is regenerated every frame by voxelizing the scene and propagating distances via Eikonal equation approximation. To enable this project, I created an in-house render hardware interface (RHI) on top of Vulkan.

Shape Modeling Language (ShapeML)

2018–2021

ShapeML is my own rule- or grammar-based procedural modeling library and framework inspired by Shape Grammars, L-Systems, and CityEngine. It generates detailed 3D models iteratively by replacing a coarse model with more detailed components. ShapeML comes with a parser/interpreter and it ships with an interactive OpenGL renderer that forward/deferred rendering, rendering of transparency objects (via weighted blended order-independent transparency, a layered fragment buffer, or adaptive transparency), several tone mapping methods, sorted and batched draw calls, optimized PCF cascade shadows maps, physically-based rendering (PBR), fast approximate anti-aliasing (FXAA), and scalable ambient obscurance, …

Code and details are on Github: https://github.com/stefalie/shapeml.

2020

Design Transformations for Rule-based Procedural Modeling

S. Lienhard, C. Lau, P. Müller, P. Wonka, M. Pauly

2017 — Computer Graphics Forum (Proc. Eurographics)

pdf, supplemental material, DOI

Fine-grained rule merging allows combining components of several rule/grammer-based procedural models to generate many design variations and to create animated transformation sequences between different procedural designs.

Interactive Design of Probability Density Functions for Shape Grammars

M. Dang, S. Lienhard, D. Ceylan, B. Neubert, P. Wonka, and M. Pauly

2015 — ACM Transaction on Graphics (Proc. SIGGRAPH Asia)

pdf, supplemental material, DOI

From a user's preference scores, Gaussian process regression learns a probability distribution function for a stochastic procedural grammar. Rule splitting operations adapt the grammar to generate models according to that distribution.

A Full Bi-Tensor Neural Tractography Algorithm Using the Unscented Kalman Filter

S. Lienhard, J. Malcolm, C.-F. Westin, and Y. Rathi

2011 — EURASIP Journal on Advances in Signal Processing

pdf, DOI

My MSc project at the Laboratory of Mathematics in Imaging (Harvard Med School, Boston) was on visualizing neural fiber bundles in human brains. Dominant directions of water movement lie along neural paths and are traced in diffusion tensor MRI data with an unscented Kalman filter. My project was later on extended by other researchers and lives on in 3D Slicer.

Orxonox

2005–2006

Orxonox was an open source space shooter game developed by students at ETH Zurich. I contributed a water shader with waves, transparency, reflections and refractions written in OpenGL and GLSL, and I worked on an ffmpeg-based in-game video player together with a colleague. Unfortunately, videos and screenshots of my work have been lost to time.
